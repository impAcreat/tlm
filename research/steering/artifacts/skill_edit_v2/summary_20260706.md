## Current conclusion

Step 1 is validated: same-class manual new skill strongly improves task success over old under the target vLLM evaluator.

Step 2 extraction is implemented and tested for both NPM-style inter-trajectory and intra-trajectory signals.

Step 2 rollout steering is not yet a positive task-level result. Inter-trajectory steering currently worsens repetition. Intra-step steering shows a small repeated-action reduction, but no success improvement.

Most likely next fix: unify activation model with the vLLM target model by loading `models/Qwen3.5-4B` under HF, or expose hidden-state steering for the same served model. After model unification, rerun a small alpha sweep with generation-only injection rather than all-token injection.

Current Qwen3.5 conclusion:
- The old/new direct skill positive-control is valid on the same HF model used for steering.
- Current NPM-style mean-diff vectors plus residual injection do not transfer the textual skill edit into task success.
- The best signal is modest trajectory hygiene improvement with inter trajectory, last-token, alpha `1`: repeated actions `26 -> 22`, success unchanged at `1/6`.
- The next method change should not be more alpha sweeps. It should change vector construction or injection timing, e.g. extract vectors at action-generation positions only, use paired same-task old/new hidden states, or use a contrastive classifier/objective rather than raw mean difference over rendered trajectory text.
